Spoilage

Food spoilage is the process whereby food becomes unsuitable and canâ€™t be eaten. Bacterias and various fungies are the causes of spoilage and can create serious consequences for consumers.

Types of Spoilage

Microbial spoilage

Microbial spoilage is caused by microorganisms like fungi (molds, yeasts) and bacteria.

Solution for Microbial Spoilage

Solutions to prevent microbial spoilage include:

  • Proper refrigeration
  • Use of preservatives
  • Pasteurization
  • Hygienic processing
  • Vacuum packaging

Physical spoilage

Physical spoilage is due to physical damage to food during harvesting, processing, or distribution.

Solution for Physical Spoilage

Solutions to prevent physical spoilage include:

  • Careful handling during harvesting
  • Proper storage and packaging
  • Avoiding mechanical damage
  • Gentle transportation
  • Using cushioning material

Chemical spoilage

Oxidation, hydrolysis, and the Maillard reaction spoil food by changing its color, flavor, and texture.

Solution for Chemical Spoilage

Solutions to prevent chemical spoilage include:

  • Protecting food from oxygen
  • Using antioxidants
  • Storing in cool, dark places
  • Moisture control
  • Preventing metal contamination
