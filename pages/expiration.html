Expired Food & Expired Date

There are two types of data, which are “sell by” and “ use by”.

Expired Food - Expired food refers to food products that have passed their “use by” or “expiration date”

Expired Date - The dates you see on food packaging write with (EXP) mean the expired date and that show how long food maintains the best taste and texture.

Expired Date Label

Consider these things before you buy ready-made products.

Use By

"Use-by" : Date is the last date recommended for the use of the product while at peak quality. It is not a safe date except when used on infant formula.

Sell By

"Sell-by" : Date tells the store how long to display the product for sale for inventory management. It is not a safe date.

Unique Ways to Use "Expired" Food

Before you toss it, try these awesome ideas!

Old Coffee Grounds
Make body scrub! Mix with coconut oil,teaspoon of salt and sugar for soft skin.
Expired Flour
Create a natural glue which is more safer to use for children.
Sour Milk
Perfect for yogurt! The acidity makes the taste even better. You can also make for cleaning product like spray for windows.
Brown Avocados
Mash for hair masks or face moisturizer. Full of healthy fats for your skin!
Stale Bread
Make croutons, bread pudding, or French toast. Dry for breadcrumbs!
