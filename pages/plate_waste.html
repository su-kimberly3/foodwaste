Plate Waste

Plate Waste is also known as a post-consumer food waste.
Plate waste refers to the portion of the food that is uneatten leftover on a plate.

Plate Waste is mostly seen in restaurant because some coustomer may not eat their food due to the portion of food.

Portion Sizes in Plate waste

There were lots of customers who said the portion was too big and resulting in leftover food.The study of WRAP, shows that half 48% of people report that portion size is the biggest reason they leave food when eating out .
On the other hand, 15% of main courses left uneaten. This leftover food makes the customerâ€™s money wasted. In order to control the leftover and plate waste, the restaurant must offer the pate portion guide.

For Example

  • Vegetables: half a plate
  • Protein: quarter of a plate
  • Carbs: quarter of a plate
  • High fat foods: small portion in the center of the plate

Using a plate as a guide for portion control can control the food portion and may even be divided with a well-balanced meal.

How can we reuse plate waste food?

  • Compost
  • Feed the pets
  • Reuse to make a snack
  • Taking a smaller portion size
