Production Food Waste

Production food waste is also known as Manufacturing waste. Production food waste(Manufacturing waste) is anything that is not used or is thrown away when making products. This can include extra raw materials, leftover pieces, and broken or damaged items. It also includes things like energy or water that are used but not needed. All of this waste can cost money and harm the environment.

Types of Production Food Waste

Overproduction

Produce the products more than we need , it is like surplus.

Transport

Moving parts and products in several times

Processing

Performing unnecessary steps in manufacturing of your products.

Inventory

Overstock leads to surplus in warehouse and inventory management

Tips to Reduce Production Food Waste

Set a waste management goal

Once you set a goal, it can help your team to stay on path and how well your company is wasting food.

Minimize overstock

Producing over products can lead to significant waste. To prevent overstocking, just manufacture products when needed.

Set up a maintenance schedule

This can help to prevent the unexpected breakdown or damage machinery, and also ensuring that your production process continues.

Reduce packaging

By reducing the plastic packaging use and use biodegradable can highly benefits your business and environment.

Optimize your shop floor

This means they can have effective and fast communication on the shop floor that can maintain value and prevent waste.
